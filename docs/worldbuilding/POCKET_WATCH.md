# Pocket Watch — Worldbuilding Bible

> *"Time is just another territory to control."* — Tre

---

## Overview

**Pocket Watch** is a multidimensional crime thriller following **Tre**, a ruthless street-level gangster from the 1990s who discovers his antique pocket watch can punch holes through dimensional fabric. Tasked — by forces he barely understands — with assassinating a Nazi leader embedded deep inside the 1990s underworld, Tre must navigate mob hierarchies, drug economies, temporal paradoxes, and alternate versions of himself across an ever-branching multiverse.

---

## 1. The Universe / Dimension System

### Primary Timeline — **Dimension α (Alpha)**
- **Setting**: 1990s urban America (Detroit / Chicago composite).
- **Status**: Baseline reality. Tre's point of origin.
- **Characteristics**: Standard causality, mob rule, crack-cocaine economy at its peak, racial tension, corrupt police.

### Alternate Dimensions

| ID | Name | Key Divergence | Status |
|----|------|---------------|--------|
| β (Beta) | *The Freeze* | The Cold War never ended — Soviet proxy state controls the Midwest | Stable |
| γ (Gamma) | *The Golden Empire* | The Black Panthers won their war and established an autonomous nation-state | Stable |
| δ (Delta) | *Clockwork* | Prohibition never ended — alcohol-based mob economy still dominant | Fragile |
| ε (Epsilon) | *The Reich's America* | Nazi-aligned government controls the Eastern Seaboard | **Danger Zone** |
| ζ (Zeta) | *The Void* | Dimensional collapse in progress; time moves non-linearly | Collapsing |

### Paradox Zones
Pockets of spacetime where two or more timelines have collided or overlapped. Physical laws are unstable. Multiple versions of the same person can coexist — or destroy each other.

- **The Overlap**: Downtown district where α and ε share the same physical space on Thursdays.
- **Dead Hour**: 3:17 AM — every dimension simultaneously pauses for 60 seconds of cross-dimensional bleed.
- **The Scar**: Site of the first illegal time-jump; a permanent rift in South Side that bleeds echoes of past events.

## 2. Character Profiles

### Tre — *The Traveler*
- **Role**: Protagonist
- **Origin**: Dimension α, Detroit, 1993
- **Occupation**: Mid-level street enforcer for the Delacroix Family
- **Ability**: Bonded with the Pocket Watch — a pre-Civil War relic that responds only to him
- **Mission**: Assassinate **Oberstmann** before he consolidates Nazi influence across Dimension ε and bleeds it into α
- **Flaw**: Ruthlessness. Tre believes in power over justice and often causes the paradoxes he's trying to prevent
- **Arc**: From chaos agent to reluctant guardian of causality

### Oberstmann — *The Target*
- **Role**: Primary Antagonist
- **Origin**: Dimension ε, 1940s (displaced forward to 1990s)
- **Occupation**: Shadow leader of a white-supremacist criminal syndicate masquerading as a private security firm
- **Ability**: Possesses a mirror artifact — the **Hour Glass** — that can freeze localized time
- **Goal**: Collapse Dimension γ (the liberated Black nation-state) and install ε's racial order as the multiverse default

### Mama Delacroix — *The Broker*
- **Role**: Mob Boss / Reluctant Ally
- **Origin**: Dimension α
- **Occupation**: Matriarch of the Delacroix crime family — controls narcotics distribution across three city sectors
- **Knowledge**: Aware of the dimensional system; has been exploiting it for smuggling for 15 years
- **Motivation**: Protecting her empire; will trade information for loyalty

### Ghost — *The Fixer*
- **Role**: Supporting / Wildcard
- **Origin**: Unknown (believed to be Dimension ζ — the collapsing void)
- **Occupation**: Dimensional mercenary; appears across timelines without aging
- **Ability**: Memory of all timelines — he has lived through hundreds of iterations
- **Motivation**: Inscrutable; may be working for forces outside the dimensional hierarchy

### Detective Sable — *The Pursuer*
- **Role**: Antagonist / Foil
- **Origin**: Dimension α
- **Occupation**: Homicide detective; unknowingly the temporal anchor that holds α stable
- **Arc**: Begins chasing Tre for a murder he committed in a now-erased timeline; eventually realizes the stakes are existential

### Tre-ε — *The Shadow Self*
- **Role**: Dimensional doppelgänger / Dark Mirror
- **Origin**: Dimension ε
- **Description**: A version of Tre who sided with Oberstmann's network. Hardened, ideologically corrupted, still recognizable
- **Role in Story**: Forces the protagonist Tre to confront what he could become

## 3. Timeline Events & Paradoxes

### Primary Event Sequence (Dimension α)

| Year | Event | Paradox Risk |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 1987 | Tre's older brother Marcus is killed in a gang hit | None — origin event |
| 1991 | Tre receives the Pocket Watch from a dying Numbers Man | Low |
| 1993 | Tre makes his first unintentional jump — lands in 1987 | **HIGH** — could save Marcus, destroying his motivation to travel |
| 1993 | Tre is recruited by Ghost for the Oberstmann mission | Medium |
| 1994 | Tre crosses into Dimension ε | High |
| 1994 | Tre confronts Tre-ε | **CRITICAL** — physical contact causes localized timeline collapse |
| 1995 | Oberstmann is assassinated (target event) | **Variable** — outcome determines multiverse stability |
| 1995+ | Post-assassination bleed effects ripple through all dimensions | TBD |

### Major Paradoxes

#### The Grandfather Paradox — "Marcus"
Tre can travel back to 1987 and save his brother Marcus. But Marcus's death is what made Tre ruthless enough to become the person the Pocket Watch bonded with. If Marcus lives, Tre becomes a different person who never receives the Watch and never completes the mission — meaning Oberstmann wins.

**Resolution Mechanic**: The Watch creates a *branch* rather than an overwrite. Saving Marcus spawns a new Dimension η where Marcus lives, but Tre's α-self continues unchanged.

#### The Bootstrap Paradox — "The Watch"
The Pocket Watch has no clear origin. In timeline after timeline, Tre *gives* the Watch to the Numbers Man in 1981, who then gives it to young Tre in 1991. The Watch was never manufactured. It simply loops.

**Resolution Mechanic**: The Watch exists as a **closed causal loop** — a stable temporal object with no true origin point.

#### The Collapse Paradox — "Tre-ε"
If Tre-α and Tre-ε occupy the same spacetime point for more than 60 seconds, the localized dimension begins to collapse as the universe tries to reconcile two versions of the same causal chain.

**Resolution Mechanic**: One version must willingly exit the dimension (jump out) or the Watch can be used to quarantine one of them in a **Paradox Zone**.

#### The Observer Paradox — "Detective Sable"
Detective Sable is unaware she is a temporal anchor. Her obsession with solving the case generates enough causal weight to stabilize Dimension α. If she ever stops investigating, α loses its anchor and becomes vulnerable to ε's bleed. Tre must keep her chasing him.

## 4. Drug Trade Economy Across Dimensions

The illicit economy functions as the nervous system of the criminal multiverse. Different dimensions have different dominant substances and power structures.

| Dimension | Primary Substance | Controller | Price Index (vs. α) | Notes |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| α | Crack cocaine / Heroin | Delacroix Family, Vipers, Eastside Crew | Baseline | Tre's home turf |
| β | Pharmaceutical-grade amphetamines | Soviet proxy cartels | 2× | Regulated channels, heavy enforcement |
| γ | Cannabis / Psychedelics (legalized) | State-adjacent cooperatives | 0.3× | Low profit — not a criminal economy |
| δ | Moonshine / Morphine | Legacy Prohibition families | 1.5× | Guns-and-liquor culture |
| ε | Methamphetamine | Aryan Brotherhood network | 3× | Oberstmann's primary funding mechanism |

**Cross-Dimensional Arbitrage**: Ghost specializes in buying cheap in γ and selling premium in ε. Mama Delacroix has been doing this quietly with heroin from β for a decade.

**The Pocket Watch as Smuggling Tool**: Before Tre understood its power, Mama Delacroix's people used it as a mule device — carrying product between dimensions hidden inside its case.

## 5. Mob Family Structures & Territorial Rules

### The Delacroix Family (Dimension α)
- **Matriarch**: Mama Delacroix
- **Underboss**: Silk (her son — ruthless, not smart)
- **Consigliere**: The Professor (academic-turned-criminal, dimensional knowledge)
- **Territories**: South Side, river docks, airport freight
- **Code**: *"We bleed together or we bleed separately."*

### The Vipers (Dimension α)
- **Boss**: King Cobra (Tre's original employer before Delacroix)
- **Territory**: West corridors, housing projects
- **Relationship to Tre**: Hostile — Tre defected, owes a blood debt

### The Forty-Eight (Dimension β)
- **Structure**: Soviet-style cell system — no single boss, collective leadership
- **Territory**: The Freeze's industrial zones
- **Relationship to Tre**: Neutral-for-hire; respect competence

### The New Canaan Brotherhood (Dimension γ)
- **Structure**: Community-based militia turned institutional
- **Territory**: The entire liberated nation-state
- **Role**: Protective; deeply suspicious of α visitors

### Oberstmann's Network (Dimension ε)
- **Structure**: Military chain of command
- **Name**: Schwarze Sonne Enterprises (front company)
- **Territorial Control**: Eastern Seaboard, political influence in three swing states
- **Weakness**: Oberstmann is irreplaceable — no succession plan

### Universal Territorial Rules
1. **No jumping into another family's territory without clearance** — applies dimensionally as well as geographically.
2. **The Dead Hour is neutral ground** — all violence pauses during the 3:17 AM bleed.
3. **Paradox Zones are no-man's land** — whoever enters loses protection from their organization.
4. **The Watch is singular** — whoever possesses it is granted temporary diplomatic immunity by anyone who understands the multiverse.

## 6. Time Travel Mechanics & Consequences

### The Pocket Watch — Mechanics
- **Activation**: Requires specific emotional state (acute stress, grief, or rage) from its bonded user
- **Navigation**: The inner dial sets destination year; the outer ring selects dimension by a symbology only Tre can read
- **Range**: ±50 years from current position; inter-dimensional jumps cost more energy
- **Cost**: Each jump ages Tre by a variable amount (usually hours, occasionally days). Extended use could be fatal.
- **Limitation**: Cannot jump to a moment where Tre already exists — creates instant Collapse Paradox

### Consequence Tiers

| Action | Consequence | Reversibility |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Observing a past event without interfering | Minimal — dimensional memory echo | Fully reversible |
| Minor interference (conversation, picking up an object) | Timeline branch — low probability | Reversible within 24 hours |
| Major interference (preventing a death, altering a deal) | Timeline split — new dimension spawned | Irreversible after branch stabilizes |
| Killing your own past/future self | Dimensional Collapse of local zone | Irreversible — Paradox Zone created permanently |
| Destroying the Watch | All jumps Tre ever made simultaneously unravel | Catastrophic — theoretical only |

### Rules of Survival
1. **Don't change what you remember** — if you remember an event happening, it happened. Changing it creates a branch, not an erasure.
2. **The Watch remembers** — it logs every jump in a sequence of internal engravings that appear after each use
3. **Bring nothing back** — objects carried across dimensions degrade and become unstable within 72 hours
4. **Never meet yourself** — even eye contact across a crowded room can begin causal distortion

## 7. Database Schema (Conceptual)

```
Dimension
    └── has many Timelines
    └── has many Characters (via CharacterState)

Timeline
    └── belongs to Dimension
    └── has many Events
    └── has many Paradoxes

Character
    └── has many CharacterStates (one per Dimension they've visited)
    └── has many Events (as participant)

Event
    └── belongs to Timeline
    └── has many Characters (participants)
    └── may trigger Paradox

Paradox
    └── belongs to Timeline
    └── references triggering Event
    └── has resolution status
    └── may collapse Timeline
```

| Endpoint | Method | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|

| `/api/pocket-watch/paradoxes/` | GET | All paradoxes with resolution status |

| `/api/pocket-watch/jump/` | POST | Simulate a time jump (returns consequences) |
